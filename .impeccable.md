## Design Context

### Users
OpenSocial is for people with fragmented digital social lives who want to turn a concrete social intent into a real human connection quickly. Core contexts include urban professionals, gamers, hobby and sports users, creators, and builders using chat as the primary interaction model.

### Brand Personality
Modern, agentic, minimal. The interface should feel fast, capable, and structurally clear rather than decorative. It should communicate intelligence and momentum without looking noisy, playful, or overdesigned.

### Aesthetic Direction
The product should feel modern and minimal with an agentic point of view: clear hierarchy, restrained surfaces, direct language, and focused workflows. It should explicitly avoid generic AI aesthetics such as overused glassmorphism, decorative gradient treatments, card-grid dashboards, and flashy “futuristic” chrome that does not serve the task.

### Design Principles
1. Intent first: every surface should help users act on a concrete social goal.
2. Agentic, not ornamental: the UI should feel capable because workflows are coherent, not because effects are flashy.
3. Minimal with hierarchy: use fewer elements, but make state, navigation, and action priority unmistakable.
4. Preserve core shells: extend the existing home, chats, and profile surfaces without redesign drift.
5. Operational clarity over decoration: loading, realtime, and system states should be easy to understand and debug.
