# 01 — Product PRD

## Problem

### Current user behavior
When people want to do something social, they currently:
1. search across fragmented platforms
2. find stale groups or dead communities
3. post manually into the void
4. wait for uncertain responses
5. message multiple people one by one
6. coordinate manually
7. often give up

### Core pain
The cost of turning “I want to do X” into “I’m doing X with someone” is too high.

### User examples
- I want to talk about yesterday’s football match.
- I want someone to play table tennis with tonight.
- I want a gaming duo now.
- I just moved to a city and want people into startups and fitness.
- I want to practice English with someone available this evening.
- I want to find 3 people for a poker game.
- I’m bored tonight and want something low-friction and social.

## Solution
A conversational interface where users express intent in natural language, the system identifies relevant and available candidates, sends explicit opt-in requests, and opens direct human chat or a group space only after acceptance.

## Target users
### Initial wedge
- urban professionals
- gamers
- sports / hobby users
- creators / builders
- people with active but fragmented digital social lives

### Behavioral fit
- values speed
- values specificity
- wants lower social friction
- does not want to browse endlessly
- is comfortable with chat as primary interface

## Core jobs to be done
- Help me find someone to talk to about a topic now.
- Help me find people to do a specific activity.
- Help me quickly assess whether people are actually available and interested.
- Help me avoid blasting multiple groups or DMs.
- Help me form a small group around a specific plan.

## Core product principles
1. Intent first
2. Explicit consent always
3. Human conversation remains human
4. The system should feel fast and concrete
5. No feed debt
6. Safety is structural, not added later
7. Deterministic rails around agentic behavior

## Initial scope
### In
- 1:1 text chat
- activity-based intent
- topic-based intent
- request / accept / decline flow
- profile creation
- profile picture
- Google login
- trust and reporting
- basic availability states
- async notifications
- real-time chat and presence

### Out
- public feed
- stories/posts
- autonomous AI socializing
- group voice/video
- direct third-party community integrations in V1
- cross-platform scraping of external communities

## Success criteria for v1
- users understand the product in < 30 seconds
- median time to routed candidates < 10 seconds
- median time to first accepted request < 5 minutes in healthy cohorts
- request acceptance rate sufficient to generate repeat usage
- safety/reporting baseline in place before scale
